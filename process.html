Our Process

Discover the ancient art of mezcal production, where time-honored traditions meet innovative sustainable practices.

Every bottle tells the story of time-honored traditions and innovative techniques passed down through generations of master mezcaleros.

1

Sowing & Harvesting

We only harvest mature agaves. Sowing agaves in our own lands at Oaxaca's Valley. Our espadin agaves grow for 8-10 years and up to 20 years for some wild varieties. Plants are handpicked once they have reached maturity without the use of pesticides or chemicals.

2

Roasting

Agave hearts (piñas) are slowly roasted in our patented energy-efficient earthen oven using mesquite and ocote pine for 3-5 days. Eric's oven design reduces cooking time and wood consumption while creating a more environmentally friendly process.

3

Fermentation

Natural open-air fermentation in Sabino (Kapok) tree barrels with mountain spring water. The exotic wood and mountain water are essential to the unique flavor profile. No additives — all flavors derive from agave and the natural process.

4

Double Distillation

First distillation in steel to concentrate, then refined in copper pot stills under Eric's supervision. His patented technique softens the smoke to let the natural agave notes dominate.
